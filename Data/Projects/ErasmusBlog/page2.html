Erasmus+ Experience Dissemination Plan

Enhancing knowledge, building networks, and creating impact through Erasmus+ BIP participation.

Overview of Erasmus+ Project Dissemination

Dissemination of project results is a mandatory phase for any Erasmus+ initiative, designed to enhance project visibility and impact.

Key objectives of dissemination include:

  • Increasing project visibility and awareness;
  • Promoting best practices for future applicants;
  • Enhancing the implementing organization’s profile;
  • Establishing partnerships for future initiatives;
  • Strengthening visibility of the Erasmus+ program.

Effective Communication and Dissemination

A strategic approach is essential for maximizing the reach of Erasmus+ results. The following elements are integral:

  • Communication Goals: Raising awareness, promoting values, fostering partnerships.
  • Target Audience: General public, stakeholders, experts, decision-makers, media.
  • Channels and Activities: Social media, events, publications, and digital outreach.
  • Project Results: Practical tools, research, and new skills acquired during the project.
  • Timeline: Planning milestones linked to project phases with flexibility for evolving needs.
  • KPIs: Key indicators to monitor and measure progress and success.

Local and International Dissemination

Dissemination begins within the host institution and extends locally and internationally. At the international level, partners promote the project in their countries and across the EU, while locally, activities are tailored to the community's unique characteristics, ensuring impactful and relevant engagement.

Methods of Dissemination

Various channels ensure a broad and tailored reach:

  • Organization's website
  • Social media platforms: Facebook, Twitter, Instagram (using #erasmus+ )
  • Audio/Video materials
  • Media press releases and appearances
  • Conferences, seminars, and training events
  • Publications, brochures, and custom project visuals (e.g., logo)

Live Sharing on Social Media

Throughout the Erasmus+ journey, experiences will be shared live on multiple platforms to enhance real-time engagement:

  • Instagram: Stories and photo updates highlighting the cultural exchange experience.
  • Facebook: Weekly summaries and reflections.
  • LinkedIn: Professional insights to reach a broader network of colleagues and professionals.
